Europa:

Europa is one of the largest natural satellites of Jupiter and is one of the most interesting moons in the Solar System. Scientists study Europa because they believe it has a huge ocean of liquid water beneath its icy surface. This makes Europa one of the best places to search for signs of possible life beyond Earth. It also helps scientists learn more about how planets and moons form and change over time.

Scientists believe Europa formed about 4.5 billion years ago from the cloud of gas and dust that surrounded Jupiter when the planet was forming. Over time, gravity pulled this material together to create the moon. Europa is made mostly of rock and metal in its centre, with a thick outer layer of water ice. Beneath the ice is a large ocean of liquid water that is kept from freezing by heat produced inside the moon.

Europa has a smooth, bright surface made of ice with very few craters. Instead of many impact marks, its surface is covered with long, dark cracks and lines caused by the movement of the ice. Scientists think these cracks form because Jupiter's gravity stretches and squeezes Europa, causing the icy surface to shift. Europa has a diameter of about 3,122 kilometres, making it slightly smaller than Earth's Moon. It orbits Jupiter at a distance of about 671,000 kilometres and takes about 3.5 Earth days to complete one orbit.
